Pipestave Pollinator Garden

693 Main St, West Newbury, MA 01985

At the entrance to Pipestave Hill, a pollinator habitat greets visitors on the right. The garden was designed around a red oak that had been planted in 1998 as part of a prior garden which had not been maintained over the years. Establishing the space entailed in the first year filling a ditch in the back with compost and leaves. Cardboard was used to suppress grass and non-natives and a layer of mulch spread over the entire space. Native shrubs and perennials were chosen for their pollinator values and purchased from nurseries specializing in natives and from native perennials grown from seed by WN2 volunteers.
